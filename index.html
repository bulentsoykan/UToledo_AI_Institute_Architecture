The physical and human resources that make the research matrix work — four stacked layers from hardware up to people.

Layer 1 · Research Compute Infrastructure

Hardware

Local AI Clusters

On-campus GPU servers for prototyping, training, and sensitive data that cannot leave campus.

State & National Grid

OARnet & OSC fiber to Ohio Supercomputer Center and Oak Ridge National Lab for massive-scale jobs.

Commercial Cloud

AWS & Azure for hosted models, public-facing tools, and scalable storage.

The Missing Fuel

Isolated, HIPAA-grade environments for clinical and federal defense data.

Centralized Repositories

Curated datasets — Lake Erie water, manufacturing sensors — available to UToledo researchers.

Data Governance Policy

Strict rules for ownership, privacy, and sharing.

Software

MLOps

Cross-College Growth
Expanding the Coalition

The four pillars are anchored in engineering, medicine, and the natural sciences — but AI maturity at UToledo will require every college. Two have already raised their hands; several others have natural on-ramps. Each brings a distinct kind of growth to the institute.

Judith Herb College of Arts, Social Sciences and Education

Active interest
  • Digital humanities — computational analysis of historical archives, literature, and Toledo’s civic records.
  • Ethics & society — philosophy and communication faculty anchor the human-context side of the Trustworthy AI horizontal.
  • K–16 AI literacy — education programs translate research into teacher-prep curricula and classroom modules.
  • Generative arts & media — visual, music, and theater faculty for AI as exhibition, performance, and design.
  • Spatial & social science — Geography & Planning brings GIS and urban analytics to the Environmental and Manufacturing verticals.

What they grow: the human-context layer the matrix needs to land in real classrooms, communities, and cultural institutions — plus the policy and ethics depth that makes federal Trustworthy-AI grants competitive.

John B. and Lillian E. Neff College of Business and Innovation

Active interest
  • Applied business analytics — the new online M.A.B.A. program is a natural feeder of trained students into research and industry partnerships.
  • Finance, risk & ESG modeling — fraud detection, market microstructure, climate-risk pricing.
  • Supply chain & operations — direct partner to the Advanced Manufacturing & Logistics vertical.
  • Information systems & HR analytics — workforce modeling, organizational adoption of AI tools.
  • Innovation & tech transfer — commercialization pathways that turn faculty research into licensable products and regional jobs.

What they grow: the commercialization engine that converts research models into revenue, plus the analytics workforce regional employers are already asking for.

College of Law

Natural on-ramp
  • AI policy & governance — framing for Pillar 1’s policy committee.
  • IP, data licensing & liability — clears legal questions around training data, model artifacts, and clinical deployment.

What they grow: the legal scaffolding that lets sensitive research actually leave the lab.

College of Health and Human Services

Natural on-ramp
  • Public-health AI — population-scale modeling of social determinants and access.
  • Social work & behavioral data — case-load analytics with strict ethical guardrails, complementing the Healthcare vertical from a community-services angle.

What they grow: the population-health complement to UTMC’s clinical work — the “outside the hospital” half of healthcare AI.

College of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences

Already active in matrix
  • Drug discovery & pharmacogenomics — foundation-model-driven candidate screening and patient-specific therapy modeling.
  • Clinical pharmacy informatics — integrating with the EHR and clinical decision support work.

What they grow: a molecular-to-bedside pipeline that broadens the Healthcare vertical beyond imaging and notes.

University College

Reach & workforce
  • Stackable AI micro-credentials — certificates for working professionals who do not need a full degree.
  • Adult & continuing education — the institute’s primary channel for regional workforce reskilling.

What they grow: the institute’s reach into Northwest Ohio’s existing workforce — the constituency that drives state-level political and funding support.

Why this structure. It separates the academic mission from the research engine while building a bridge between them, ensures faculty have the hardware and human support they need to succeed, and leaves explicit on-ramps for every UToledo college to fold in as the coalition grows.
