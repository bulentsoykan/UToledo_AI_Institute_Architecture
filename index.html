4
The Foundation
Infrastructure & Enablement

The physical and human resources that make the research matrix work — four stacked layers from hardware up to people.

Layer 1 · Research Compute Infrastructure

Hardware

Local AI Clusters

On-campus GPU servers for prototyping, training, and sensitive data that cannot leave campus.

State & National Grid

OARnet & OSC fiber to Ohio Supercomputer Center and Oak Ridge National Lab for massive-scale jobs.

Commercial Cloud

AWS & Azure for hosted models, public-facing tools, and scalable storage.

Layer 2 · Research Data Architecture

The Missing Fuel

Secure Data Enclaves

Isolated, HIPAA-grade environments for clinical and federal defense data.

Centralized Repositories

Curated datasets — Lake Erie water, manufacturing sensors — available to UToledo researchers.

Data Governance Policy

Strict rules for ownership, privacy, and sharing.

Layer 3 · AI Systems Design, Analysis Engineering & Deployment

Software

MLOps

Code versioning, model monitoring, and reproducible research workflows.

Software Engineering Support

Dedicated staff who wrap research models in clean interfaces so partners (hospitals, businesses) can actually use them.

Layer 4 · Research Faculty Enablement

Human Support

Data Science Consulting

"Office hours" pairing domain experts with AI experts to apply ML to specific lab work.

Grant Development Support

Help drafting the technical computational-plan sections of NSF, NIH, and DOE proposals.

Training Workshops

Boot camps on local hardware, cloud platforms, and secure data enclaves.

Why this structure. It separates the academic mission from the research engine while building a bridge between them, and it ensures faculty have the hardware and human support they need to succeed.
